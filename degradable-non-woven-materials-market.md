<p><ul><li>Spunlaced Nonwovens</li><li>Melt-blown Nonwovens</li><li>Spunbond Nonwovens</li><li>Needle-punched Nonwovens</li><li>Others</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The degradable non-woven materials market comprises several types, including spunlaced, melt-blown, spunbond, and needle-punched nonwovens. </p><p>Spunlaced nonwovens use high-pressure water jets to entangle fibers, offering softness and strength. Melt-blown nonwovens are produced by extruding melted fibers, providing excellent filtration properties. Spunbond nonwovens involve continuous filament fibers, resulting in high durability. Needle-punched nonwovens are created by mechanically entangling fibers with needles, enhancing density and strength. Other types may include specialty nonwovens designed for specific applications, contributing to the market's diverse offerings.</p></p>
