<p><ul><li>Starch-based Fuel Ethanol</li><li>Sugar-based Fuel Ethanol</li><li>Cellulosic Fuel Ethanol</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The fuel ethanol market is categorized into three main types: starch-based, sugar-based, and cellulosic. Starch-based fuel ethanol is derived from crops like corn and wheat, utilizing starch conversion processes. Sugar-based fuel ethanol, primarily sourced from sugarcane and sugar beets, involves fermentation of sugars. Cellulosic fuel ethanol is produced from non-food biomass, such as wood and agricultural residues, through advanced enzymatic and microbial processes. Each type plays a distinct role in renewable energy production and sustainability efforts.</p></p>
